About me

My name is Jude Samuel, and I was born in Abuja, the capital of Nigeria and I’m from the Igbo tribe. My journey began in the suburbs of the city where I spent my early years surrounded by a loving christian family, friends and nature. Growing up in a bustling but average household with four siblings, life was always an adventure. In those early years, I was fascinated by the ever-evolving world of technology, despite limited access. I spent countless hours exploring the digital realm with the devices available at that time, dismantling old electronics, and piecing together little domestic devices like torches and toys. I vividly remember taking apart my first toy truck given to me, by my aunt, as a gift to see how it worked, only to spend hours trying to put it back together. Even though I wasn’t successful, these early experiences instilled in me a strong work ethic and a deep appreciation for the power of knowledge, even amidst the circumstances. Through trial and error, I learned the importance of perseverance and the satisfaction of overcoming challenges. I had my basic education there in Abuja and while I was fortunate to have access to quality education within the city, my experiences was widely confined to the familiar surrounding of Abuja. This has instilled in me a deep yearning to explore the world beyond my immediate environment. I am eager to travel, to immerse myself in different cultures, and to broaden my horizons through new experiences. Today, I am driven by a desire to create something that helps the whole world and contribute to the development and advancement of the future.
